STOCK PRICE FORECASTING


The main goal of this project is to improve the accuracy and efficiency of stock price predictions by integrating advanced deep learning techniques with financial market analysis. Traditional stock prediction models often rely solely on historical stock data, which can miss important contextual factors that influence price movements, such as news events, geopolitical changes, and economic indicators. Our model, however, goes a step further by using a hybrid approach that combines both stock price data and financial news articles to build a more comprehensive and robust predictive model.

Integration of Stock Data and News Articles: The key difference between our model and other traditional stock prediction models is the inclusion of textual data from financial news. By incorporating news articles related to specific companies or the broader market, our model can capture the emotional and psychological factors that drive market volatility. This approach helps predict stock prices more accurately by analyzing not only historical trends but also the public sentiment and market reactions reflected in news stories. To achieve this, we use a composite information mixing module that integrates time-series data (i.e., historical stock prices) and news text features (i.e., sentiment analysis and keywords from news articles). This hybrid model allows for the combination of quantitative data (such as closing prices, stock volume, and technical indicators) and qualitative data (such as sentiment from news reports, public perception, and economic forecasts).

Our prediction model uses several powerful deep learning models to capture various aspects of stock price movement. ARIMA (Autoregressive Integrated Moving Average): ARIMA is a classical statistical method used for time-series forecasting. It helps capture the trends, seasonality, and noise in historical stock data. ARIMA is particularly effective for short-term price predictions when historical data is available, and it forms a strong foundation for the prediction process. LSTM (Long Short-Term Memory): LSTM is a type of recurrent neural network (RNN) capable of learning long-term dependencies. In the context of stock price prediction, LSTM models are used to learn patterns in the time-series data of stock prices. The ability of LSTMs to remember important features over long sequences helps predict the impact of past prices on future movements, making them an ideal model for financial markets. GRU (Gated Recurrent Unit): GRU is another variant of RNNs similar to LSTM but with a simpler architecture. It is computationally more efficient and works well with sequences that have varying time dependencies. GRUs are particularly useful when handling large datasets with complex patterns. BiLSTM (Bidirectional LSTM): BiLSTM takes the LSTM model a step further by processing the data in both forward and backward directions. This approach allows the model to capture both future and past information when predicting stock prices, improving the accuracy of predictions when analyzing time-series data.

Text Data Processing: To extract semantic features from news articles, we use natural language processing (NLP) techniques, including sentiment analysis, tokenization, and feature extraction methods. The text is processed to identify key information such as: Sentiment Analysis: This involves determining whether the news articles reflect positive, negative, or neutral sentiment toward a particular stock or market trend. Positive news might correlate with an increase in stock prices, while negative sentiment could indicate a potential drop. Keyword Extraction: By identifying recurring keywords and themes in news articles, we can spot emerging trends, market shifts, or key events that could influence stock prices. The processed text features are then combined with the numerical stock data to form a feature set used for training our model.

Conclusion: This project demonstrates the significant advantages of integrating diverse data sources (stock prices and news articles) to create a more robust and accurate stock price prediction model. By applying advanced deep learning techniques, including ARIMA, LSTM, GRU, BiLSTM, and MLP, the model not only leverages time-series features but also incorporates semantic features from news text, allowing it to capture the complexities of the financial markets. The approach offers practical value to investors, analysts, and financial institutions, providing them with better tools for predicting market trends and making data-driven decisions in an increasingly volatile stock market.
